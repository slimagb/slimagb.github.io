Frequently Asked Questions

What is your current research focus?
I'm pursuing a PhD at LTISI lab with a focus on hybrid statistical-AI spatio-temporal models for climate-sensitive vector-borne disease surveillance in West Africa. My work combines machine learning, deep learning, and classical statistical approaches to better understand and predict disease patterns.
Are you open to collaboration or consulting?
Yes! I'm open to research collaborations, data science consulting, and speaking engagements. Whether you're looking to work on climate-health research, data engineering projects, or need analytical expertise, I'd love to discuss possibilities. Feel free to reach out via email or LinkedIn.
What tools and technologies do you use most?
Data Science & ML: Python (pandas, scikit-learn, TensorFlow), SQL, R, Stata, SPSS

Analytics & BI: Power BI (DAX), Git

Data Engineering: Apache Spark, Apache Airflow, dbt, Snowflake, Databricks, Azure Synapse
What is your teaching experience?
I've been a Teaching Assistant at Université de Thiès since August 2022. I teach macroeconomics, microeconomics, and statistics. I also lead practical sessions in Python, Stata, R, and Excel, and supervise student theses. My focus is on making complex quantitative concepts accessible and applicable.
Where can I find your publications?
You can find my publications and projects on my Works page, Google Scholar, and my GitHub for code and research implementations.
What types of consulting projects have you worked on?
At BDA Institute, I've led consulting projects including: social climate studies (BCEA), mystery shopping evaluations (CMS), market research for Lagardère Retail, political analysis (Diomaye-Sonko 100 days), and banking sector studies (UBA). I've also worked on impact evaluations at AKOME/ACET and CRDES, combining rigorous methodology with practical insights.
